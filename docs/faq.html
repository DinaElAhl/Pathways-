Frequently asked questions

# Privacy & data

Is Pathways FERPA / GDPR-K compliant?

Pathways itself is a directory — it does not collect or process student data. Individual apps in the directory vary. Each app listing indicates whether the developer has self-attested to FERPA, GDPR, or GDPR-K handling. For any deployment, review the app's own DPA (Data Processing Agreement) before onboarding students.

Do apps on Pathways train AI models on student work?

Apps that use AI must disclose it clearly. The directory's submission review checks for: whether user inputs are used for model training, whether data is retained after deletion, and whether minors' data is segregated. See the School AI Use Policy Starter.

Where is data stored?

Depends on the app. Look for the "Data residency" field on each app's detail page. EU-hosted options are filterable via the eu-hosted tag.

# Licensing

Are the resources free to use in my classroom?

Every resource entry includes a license field. Open licenses (CC BY, CC BY-SA, CC0, MIT, Apache 2.0) mean you can remix and share within the terms of that license. Others may be "all rights reserved" — usable but not redistributable.

Can I submit a commercial app?

Yes. Pathways lists both free and paid apps. The submission must be honest about pricing, including whether student accounts are billed separately.

What license are Pathways's own templates under?

Pathways-authored templates and checklists (anything attributed to "Pathways" as author) are released under CC BY 4.0 unless otherwise noted. Attribution: "Pathways, CC BY 4.0."

# LMS & SIS integration

Which LMSs do Pathways apps support?

Apps tagged lti-1.3 can launch from any LTI 1.3 conformant LMS — Canvas, Moodle 4+, Blackboard Ultra, D2L Brightspace, Schoology, and Google Classroom (via LTI add-on). See Frameworks: Interoperability.

Can I roster students automatically?

Yes — via OneRoster 1.2. Attendly and Timetabler read rosters directly; other apps can consume rostering data via the Pathways API.

What about xAPI?

Apps tagged xapi emit statements to an LRS of your choice. InsightsLED can be used as your LRS front-end if you don't have one.

# Contributing

How do I submit an app or resource?

See Submitting your work in the getting-started guide. In short: fork the repo, append a JSON entry, open a PR.

How are submissions reviewed?

Volunteer reviewers — teachers, ed-tech engineers, and accessibility specialists — check each submission against the review checklist. Typical review turnaround is under a week.

I found a broken link or incorrect info. How do I report it?

Open an issue on the public repo, or email docs@pathways.example.

Still stuck?

The getting-started guide covers the tour; the frameworks reference covers the specs; the API reference covers the programmatic surface. If the answer isn't in any of those, write to us.
